MakeMIT2015

Phase 1

After 15 hours of hacking, teams displayed their projects in an Expo for judging.

Phase 1 Winners

YouLock

Team WeLock
Atif Javed, Jen Cardona, Tristan Paine, Fiona Paine, Farhan Khan
Bike locks are heavy, cumbersome, and riders often lose their keys. YouLock is a fixed locking mechanism installed on bike racks that allows bikers to lock, unlock, and find their bike. It uses the Intel Edison and Twilio SMS to pair a phone number with a rack and trigger the locking mechanism with a mechanical servo. It includes a security feature that notifies you when your bike is being tampered with. Say goodbye to annoying locks for good!

Force Feel'd

Team 16
Everardo Rosales, Joshua Sloane, Meghana Bhat, Thomas Needham, Joanna Zhu
Virtual reality systems are becoming more commonplace and accessible, though feedback systems are miles behind. Force Feel'd brings simple, unidirectional force feedback to VR interfaces bridging mind and hand, fully immersing the user in the developer's content.

Contour Modeler

Team 3D Rock
Mitchell Gu, Jiahao Li, Travis Libsack, Joe Huang
A framework for 3D sketching that allows a user to use a "pen and paper" interface to create 3D models. We triangulate the position using ultrasonic rangefinder data from three counterweight heights sent from an Intel Edison. Our 3D visualization client allows users to draw polygons, 3D surfaces, and smooth curves depending on button input from the sketching pen.

Remaining Top Ten (Alphabetical Order)

CPR Rescue Guide

Team Heart Attackers
Alyssa Dayan, Stacy Ho, Tomohiro Maeda, Mengjiao Yang
We constructed a device (with Arduino) to guide bystanders through the process of performing CPR. The device uses a speaker to sound an alarm when the patient has a heart attack and to voice CPR instructions. The device also uses an accelerometer to provide feedback when the bystander isn't performing chest compressions hard enough.

Dancing Tensegrity Robot

Team "J" The First
Marian Styer, Joyce Chen, James Payor, Beckett Colson
Tensegrity Robot: He dances! Made primarily of acrylic, fishing line, rubber bands, and pure unadulterated passion, this is one coordinated fellow! He's even controlled by the Myo Armband!

Firefly

Team Glastonbury Landscaping LLC
Michelle Qiu, Robert Rudolph
FireFly is a lightbulb that can change warmth to adjust to a room's lighting needs. During the day, FireFly can be set to produce bright natural sunlight. At darker hours, FireFly throws a soft, warm glow that is friendlier on your eyes. Just like F.lux, for real life. A rotating collar at the base adjusts the warmth.

Handheld CNC Plotter

Team Autoplots
Marshall Craft, John La, Jack Erdozain, Elliot Owen, Jimmy Mawdsley
CNC Drawing Robot, takes in vector files and traces over drawing. Tracks motion so it can be shifted over and continue drawing if you want to draw something larger than its range of motion.

Mesh-Network Gamma Spectrometer

Team MakeRadiation
Jake Hecla, Danielius Kramnikius, Caitlin Fischer, Han Zhang, Banti Gheneti
Our device is designed to quantify and identify distributed radioisotope contamination from a wide variety of sources. This approach avoids risk to civil/military personnel and vastly improves the ability to manage evacuation routes and prioritize resources in response to nuclear disaster. In comparison to most radiation-measurement beacons, these devices have the ability to discriminate between various radioisotopes using a compact gamma spectrometer. The devices are low-cost, and can be deployed en masse via an air-drop over the target zone. Each device communicates its data through a mesh network to a master unit, which uploads it to a webserver.

Rhyno

Team Rhyno
Ahmet Can Musabeyoglu, Birkan Uzun, Wegene Tadele
We are building a Cheap, Compact and Easy to Use ring called Rhyno that detects simple gestures like swiping up, down, left and right as well as zooming in and out. It achieves this by making use of accelerometer and gyroscope sensor data. The ring also uses Bluetooth LE to communicate with any Bluetooth LE enabled devices and control them.

Securidesk

Team Crimson45
Fiorella Vargas, Jessica Lam, Joy Hui, John Holland
Pressure sensitive mat that can be activated/deactivated using RFID. When change in pressure is detected, alarm sounds, lights turned on, and user is sent an alert via email. Applications are for security of personal belongings when taking breaks (bathroom, food run, short walk or meeting) when working at library, cafe, dining hall etc.
